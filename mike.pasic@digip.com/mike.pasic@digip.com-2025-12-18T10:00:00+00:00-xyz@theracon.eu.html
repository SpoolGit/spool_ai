Handling Objection
Overcoming Trademark Objections
Objection 1: "We don't need trademark protection - we already have our company registered"

Key Arguments:

  • Company registration ≠ Trademark protection: Company registration only protects your business name locally/within your state, while trademark registration provides nationwide/international protection
  • Limited scope: Company registration doesn't protect your products, services, logos, or slogans - only trademark registration does
  • Risk of losing your name: Someone else can register YOUR company name as a trademark and legally force you to stop using it or rebrand entirely
  • No legal recourse: Without trademark registration, you cannot file federal lawsuits for infringement or stop competitors from using similar names
Objection 2: "Trademark registration is too expensive for a small business like ours"

Key Arguments:

  • Cost of NOT trademarking is higher: Rebranding can cost up to 5% of business turnover - far more expensive than registration fees ($275-325 USD for single class)
  • Avoid costly legal battles: Without trademark protection, defending your brand or resolving disputes costs significantly more in legal fees
  • Protect your most valuable asset: Your brand is often your company's most valuable asset - small investment now prevents massive losses later
  • One-time investment: Registration lasts 10+ years with renewal, providing long-term protection for a relatively small upfront cost
  • Risk of losing everything: If someone else trademarks your name first, you could lose all the brand equity and customer trust you've built

Bottom line: The cost of trademark registration is minimal compared to the cost of rebranding, legal disputes, or losing your business name entirely.
