Qualification Questions
Based on the prospect's profile and the seller's business (B2B go-to-market services company), here are the 5 most relevant qualification questions:
  1. "What are your current customer acquisition methods for entering new markets, and what challenges are you facing with your existing outbound marketing and sales efforts?"

    - This helps understand their current GTM strategy and pain points, given they're a digital marketing agency looking to potentially expand.

  2. "How are you currently measuring and optimizing your sales and marketing ROI for your clients, and what tools/processes are you using?"

    - This explores their current capabilities and potential gaps that the seller's AI-powered tools could fill.

  3. "What are your growth targets for the next 12-24 months, particularly regarding expanding into new markets or acquiring new types of clients?"

    - This helps understand their growth ambitions and if they align with the seller's ability to help enter new markets.

  4. "How do you currently handle outbound sales and lead generation for your own agency's growth, and what results are you seeing?"

    - This reveals if they're experiencing the common B2B challenge of relying too heavily on referrals/inbound, which the seller specifically addresses.

  5. "What's your typical sales cycle length and average deal size when acquiring new clients, and how satisfied are you with these metrics?"

    - This helps qualify if they fit the seller's sweet spot of "sales velocity & high ACV" and identifies potential areas for improvement.

These questions align with the seller's value proposition of combining AI tools with GTM expertise to help B2B companies enter new markets and acquire customers more efficiently.

Handling Objection
Based on the context and putting myself in the seller's shoes, here's how I would handle these objections:
  1. Budget/Cost concerns:
    • Focus on ROI and efficiency gains: Demonstrate how the investment will help them save money in the long run through improved processes and better resource allocation
    • Offer flexible pricing or payment terms that align with their budget cycles
    • Compare the cost of not implementing the solution (opportunity cost, inefficiencies, lost revenue) versus the investment required
  2. Lack of immediate need/urgency:
    • Highlight potential competitive disadvantages of maintaining status quo while competitorsevolve
    • Share relevant case studies or success stories of similar-sized agencies that have benefited from the solution
    • Focus on scalability: Even if current processes work, emphasize how the solution can help them handle future growth more efficiently
    • Create urgency by offering time-limited incentives or early adopter benefits

The key is to shift the conversation from cost to value and from current state to future potential. Keep the focus on their business objectives and how your solution helps achieve them more effectively.
