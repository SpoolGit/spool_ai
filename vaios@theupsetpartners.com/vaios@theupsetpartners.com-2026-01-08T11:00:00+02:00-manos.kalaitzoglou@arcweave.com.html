Prospect Persona

Manos Kalaitzoglou is the Founder & CEO of Arcweave, possessing a unique blend of architectural design thinking and technical entrepreneurship. Based in Athens, Greece, he is a 'visionary operator' who has led Arcweave from its first line of code to a Seed-funded ($850K) platform. His mindset is rooted in democratizing creative tools and he is deeply involved in the game development community (e.g., hosting Arcjams). Strategic and product-focused, Manos is likely the primary decision-maker for GTM strategy. He values authenticity, community engagement, and high-impact workflows that reduce friction for creative teams. He is currently focused on expanding Arcweave’s footprint from indie game development into 'Serious Games,' corporate training, and cultural heritage education.

Value Propositions
  • Predictable Revenue Engine: Transition from 'spiky' community-led growth (like jams) to a steady, predictable outbound sales motion tailored for B2B SaaS.
  • Revenue Timing Intelligence: Leverage the 'Probound' methodology to identify game studios and edtech companies precisely when they are planning new narratives—showing up before they even start looking for a tool.
  • Hands-on 'Co-founder' Support: Provide Manos with an interim GTM operator who can roll up their sleeves and execute outbound strategy, allowing the CEO to focus on high-level product and vision.
  • AI-Powered Lead Precision: Use cutting-edge AI tools to scan job descriptions and hiring patterns in the game/training industry to find the exact 'bottlenecks' that Arcweave solves.

• Company Stage: Seed-stage B2B SaaS ($850K raised in Dec 2023 from Galaxy Interactive and Genesis Ventures).

• Team Size: Small, agile team of ~8 employees based in Athens, Greece.

• Product Status: Established collaborative narrative platform with high community engagement and recent AI feature launches.

• Operating Markets: Global reach, but with a strong local presence in the Greek tech ecosystem (consistent with Vaios Gkitsas' SaaStock leadership in Athens).

  • High Relevance: Unpredictable revenue growth often associated with community-led events; lack of a repeatable sales framework ('The 4S Framework' need).
  • High Relevance: Manual effort in identifying high-value enterprise leads within the noise of a large free-user community.
  • Secondary: Limited internal resources for aggressive outbound execution while maintaining product development speed.
  • Secondary: Difficulty in proving the ROI of 'branching narratives' to enterprise CFOs (which Vaios’ 'Predictive Outbound' brief could help frame as a 'Revenue Advantage').
Qualification Questions

• Portfolio Management: How are you currently segmenting your user base between 'hobbyist/indie' and 'enterprise/serious games' prospects to prioritize sales effort?

• Pain Points: With a lean team of 8, how much of your week is spent on outbound sales versus product vision, and is that balance sustainable for your next growth phase?

• Monitoring: How do you currently identify when a game studio or corporate training department is entering a 'buying window' (e.g., project kick-off) versus just browsing tools?

• Growth Scalability: Your recent Arcjam was a huge community success, but what is the repeatable process for turning that buzz into high-value B2B contracts outside of the jam cycle?

• AI Integration: You've integrated AI into the Arcweave product; how are you using AI in your GTM stack to find and qualify leads before your competitors do?

Pre-Call Hypotheses
  • Arcweave has strong community-led growth (PLG) but lacks a structured, scalable outbound sales process for the 'Serious Games' and Enterprise sectors.
  • The CEO (Manos) is likely handling the majority of high-stakes business development personally, creating a bottleneck for scaling.
  • Arcweave's recent Seed funding means they are under pressure to show predictable revenue growth and market expansion to secure a Series A.
  • They are likely relying on word-of-mouth and inbound interest from their platform integrations (Unity/Unreal/Godot) rather than proactive, signal-based outbound.
Strategic Drivers
  • Enterprise Pivot: Moving up-market into corporate training and 'Serious Games' where contract values are significantly higher than indie plans.
  • Global Expansion: Targeting the US and UK markets (where Upset Partners has deep roots) to tap into larger game studio budgets.
  • Operational Efficiency: Automating the 'boring' parts of the sales process using AI so the lean team can focus on creative and technical excellence.
  • Market Dominance: Securing Arcweave as the industry standard for narrative design before competitors can capture the emerging 'interactive digital narrative' space in EdTech.
