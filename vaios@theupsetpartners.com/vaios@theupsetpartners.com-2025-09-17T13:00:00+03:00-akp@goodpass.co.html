Handling Objection
Based on the context and putting myself in the seller's shoes, here's how I would handle these objections:
For Budget/Cost concerns:
  1. Focus on ROI and cost savings - demonstrate how the solution can help them save money or operate more efficiently with their limited resources
  2. Offer flexible payment terms or a startup-friendly pricing tier that aligns with their current size
  3. Position it as an investment in growth rather than just an expense - show how it can help them scale efficiently as they grow from 5 employees
For Lack of proven ROI/results:
  1. Offer a pilot program or trial period so they can validate results before full commitment
  2. Share specific case studies and metrics from similar early-stage companies in their industry
  3. Break down the expected ROI into concrete metrics they care about (e.g. time saved, increased bookings, customer satisfaction scores)
  4. Propose setting clear success metrics together and tie part of the contract to achieving those goals

The key is to reduce their risk while demonstrating clear value aligned to their specific situation as a small startup. Keep the initial commitment small and focused on proving value quickly.
