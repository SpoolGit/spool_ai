Company Background

Founded in 2010 by Fanis Koutouvelis, Intale is a Greek cloud-based retail management platform that has grown to serve over 700 stores in Greece. The company has processed 40 million transactions valued at €150 million and secured $3 million in investment. Intale operates on Microsoft's Azure cloud and helps small retailers digitize operations through real-time reporting and inventory management tools.

Handling Objection

Overcoming Objections for a Small Retail Tech Company

For Budget Constraints/Cost Concerns:
  1. ROI Focus: Demonstrate clear return on investment with specific metrics relevant to retail - like increased sales conversion, reduced inventory costs, or improved customer retention.
  2. Scalable Pricing: Offer a tiered pricing structure that allows the company to start small and scale as they grow, making it more affordable for a 34-employee business.
  3. Cost of Inaction: Highlight the potential revenue loss or competitive disadvantage of not implementing the solution compared to the investment required.
  4. Phased Implementation: Suggest a staged rollout to distribute costs over time rather than requiring a large upfront investment.
For Integration Challenges:
  1. Proven Integration Track Record: Share specific examples of successful integrations with similar retail management systems.
  2. Technical Support: Emphasize dedicated integration support from your technical team throughout the implementation process.
  3. API Flexibility: Highlight your solution's API capabilities and adaptability to work with various retail systems.
  4. Minimal Disruption: Outline how the integration can be completed with minimal impact on daily operations, addressing a key concern for retail businesses.
