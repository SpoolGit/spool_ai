Handling Objection
Based on the context and putting myself in the seller's shoes, here's how I would handle these objections:
  1. Budget/Price concerns:
    • Focus on ROI and cost savings: Demonstrate how the solution will save money in the long run through improved efficiency and productivity
    • Offer flexible payment terms or a scaled-down version that can grow with their business
    • Compare the cost of not solving their current problems (inefficiencies, manual work, lost opportunities) versus investing in the solution
  2. Current solution works fine:
    • Highlight specific pain points their basic tools don't address
    • Demonstrate how the new system can help them scale efficiently as they grow beyond 18 employees
    • Offer a trial period or proof of concept to show tangible benefits without full commitment
    • Focus on future-proofing their business: "Your current solution may work now, but what about when you grow to 30 or 50 employees?"

The key is to shift the conversation from cost to value and from present state to future needs. Keep responses consultative rather than pushy, and always tie benefits back to their specific business challenges.
