Handling Objection
Based on the context and putting myself in the seller's shoes, here's how I would handle these objections:
For Price/Budget Objection:
  1. Focus on ROI and value delivered rather than cost - demonstrate how the solution saves time, reduces errors, and improves efficiency
  2. Highlight the cost of NOT implementing the solution (missed opportunities, manual work hours, potential errors)
  3. Offer flexible pricing options or implementation phases to make it more budget-friendly
  4. Compare total cost of ownership vs. maintaining current processes including hidden costs
For Integration/Implementation Concerns:
  1. Emphasize hands-on support and expertise mentioned in the context - "we roll up our sleeves and provide hands-on support"
  2. Outline a clear, phased implementation plan to minimize disruption
  3. Share success stories from similar-sized companies who've successfully implemented
  4. Offer a pilot program or staged rollout to demonstrate value while minimizing risk
  5. Highlight the availability of expert implementation support - "hands-on support from professionals who've worked with top-tier B2B companies"

The key is to shift the conversation from cost to value and from risk to opportunity, while providing concrete evidence and support to address their concerns.
