Handling Objection
Based on the context and my expertise as a sales researcher, here's how I would handle these objections:

  1. Budget/Price concerns for small startups:

    • Focus on ROI and cost savings: Demonstrate how your solution will help them save money or generate revenue in the long run
    • Offer flexible payment terms: Consider monthly payments instead of annual contracts
    • Start small: Propose a scaled-down version or pilot program that fits their budget while proving value
    • Compare costs: Show how your solution is more cost-effective than alternatives or doing nothing

  2. Multiple stakeholder buy-in:

    • Map all stakeholders early: Identify and understand the needs/priorities of both technical and business decision-makers
    • Customize presentations: Prepare different value propositions for each stakeholder (technical benefits for CTO, business impact for CEO)
    • Create consensus: Facilitate joint meetings where both parties can discuss and align on objectives
    • Provide proof points: Share relevant case studies that address both technical and business concerns
    • Build internal champions: Work with supportive stakeholders who can help advocate for your solution internally

The key is to be patient and systematic in addressing each stakeholder's specific concerns while maintaining momentum in the sales process.
