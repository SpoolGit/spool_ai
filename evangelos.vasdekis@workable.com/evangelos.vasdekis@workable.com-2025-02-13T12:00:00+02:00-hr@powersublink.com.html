Objection Handling +

Based on the context and putting myself in the seller's shoes, here's how I would handle these objections:

1. Cost/Budget concerns:

  • Emphasize the 15-day free trial with no credit card required to test the full platform
  • Focus on ROI: The automation and efficiency gains will save significant time/money in HR processes
  • Highlight how the system can "grow with you" - only pay for what you need now
  • Point out how reducing manual tasks and streamlining hiring/onboarding processes leads to cost savings in the long run

2. Integration/Technical complexity:

  • Emphasize the platform's extensive integration capabilities, including calendar sync, API access, and common tools like Zoom/Teams
  • Highlight the mobile-friendly, user-friendly interface that requires minimal technical expertise
  • Mention the available technical support and help center resources
  • Focus on how the system automates complex processes into simple workflows (approvals, document management, etc.)
  • Stress that the platform is designed to reduce complexity, not add to it, by centralizing HR processes in one place

The key is to position the solution as an investment that simplifies operations rather than a complex expense, while emphasizing the try-before-you-buy option to reduce risk.
