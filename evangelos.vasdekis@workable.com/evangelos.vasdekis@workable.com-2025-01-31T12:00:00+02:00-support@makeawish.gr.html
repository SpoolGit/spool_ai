Objection Handling +

Based on the context and putting myself in the seller's shoes, here are the key arguments to overcome these objections:

For budget constraints/non-profit:

  1. Emphasize ROI and cost savings through automation - less time spent on manual tasks means staff can focus on more valuable activities
  2. Highlight the free trial period to test the value before committing
  3. Point out that the system can grow with them, so they only pay for what they need initially
  4. Mention how the automated approval workflows and reporting can help justify spending to board members and improve budget tracking

For "manual processes are good enough":

  1. Demonstrate how even small organizations waste significant time with manual processes like interview scheduling, application tracking, and offer letters
  2. Emphasize the improved candidate experience through features like mobile-friendly applications and self-scheduling
  3. Point out that automated compliance tools help avoid costly mistakes even with low hiring volume
  4. Highlight that having proper systems in place now will make scaling easier when they grow beyond 50 employees

The key is focusing on concrete time/cost savings and risk reduction rather than just features. Keep the emphasis on solving their specific pain points rather than overwhelming them with capabilities they may not need yet.
