Objection Handling +

Based on the provided context and my expertise as a sales researcher, here's how I would handle these objections:

For Cost/Budget concerns:

  1. Focus on ROI and efficiency gains - Highlight how the software can help them save time and resources in their recruitment process, which translates to cost savings
  2. Emphasize the scalability - The solution can grow with them as they expand, making it a smart long-term investment
  3. Offer a free trial period - Let them experience the value firsthand without initial commitment
  4. Break down the cost per hire - Show how the per-hire cost becomes minimal when considering the efficiencies gained

For "Current solution working fine":

  1. Ask probing questions about their current pain points and inefficiencies in their recruitment process
  2. Highlight specific features that address common challenges for companies their size (54 employees):
    • Collaborative hiring tools to coordinate across teams
    • AI screening to reduce manual review time
    • Integration capabilities with existing tools
  3. Share relevant case studies of similar-sized companies that improved their hiring process
  4. Focus on future growth - Their current solution may work now but may not scale well as they grow

The key is to position the solution as an investment in their growth rather than just another expense, while demonstrating clear advantages over their current process through concrete examples and metrics.
