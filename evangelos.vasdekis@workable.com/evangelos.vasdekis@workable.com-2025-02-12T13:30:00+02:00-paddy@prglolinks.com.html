Objection Handling +

Based on the context and putting myself in the seller's shoes, here are the most relevant arguments to overcome these objections:

1. Cost/Budget concerns:

  • Emphasize ROI through automation and time savings ("automates process and manual tasks")
  • Offer a 15-day free trial with no credit card required to prove value
  • Highlight how it can help reduce costs through:
    • Better internal hiring (employee referral program)
    • Reduced administrative overhead
    • More efficient hiring process
    • No minimum commitments or hidden fees

2. Current system working fine:

  • Focus on pain points their current process likely has (manual tasks, spreadsheets, lack of centralization)
  • Emphasize real-time tracking and reporting capabilities that keep stakeholders informed
  • Highlight modern features their current system may lack:
    • AI-powered screening
    • Integration with 200+ job boards
    • Mobile capabilities
    • Automated approval workflows
  • Point out that "working fine" doesn't mean optimal - show how Workable can help them work better and faster

The key is to focus on concrete business value and efficiency gains rather than just features, and let them experience it risk-free through the trial period.
