    - This explores needs beyond just recruitment

These questions help qualify:

  • Decision making process

  • Readiness to evaluate new HR technology

The questions are tailored to their size, multi-location setup, and industry context while exploring needs across recruitment, employee management and payroll - the key offerings of the HR software platform.

Objection Handling +

Based on the context and putting myself in the seller's shoes, here's how I would handle these objections:

1. Cost/Budget concerns ($129/job per month is too expensive):

  • Highlight ROI: "The platform automates many manual tasks, saving significant staff time and reducing cost-per-hire"
  • Break down daily cost: "At just over $4 per day, you get access to 200+ job boards, automated screening, and advanced recruiting tools"
  • Emphasize value: "Unlike basic job posting sites, you get a complete ATS with candidate sourcing, interview scheduling, and evaluation tools all in one platform"

2. Current System/Process objection:

  • Acknowledge and probe: "I understand your current process works. What specific challenges or pain points do you face with it?"
  • Highlight automation benefits: "Workable automates approval workflows, reporting, and compliance tasks - reducing administrative burden"
  • Emphasize scalability: "Over 30,000 companies use our platform because it grows with their needs and integrates with existing tools"
  • Offer trial: "We offer a 15-day free trial so you can experience firsthand how our features complement and improve your current process"

The key is to focus on value proposition and let them experience the platform's benefits through the free trial rather than just pushing features.
