Objection Handling +

Based on the context and my expertise, here are the most effective responses to handle these common objections from small recruitment/staffing companies:

For Cost/Price Objection:

1. Focus on ROI and efficiency gains:

  • Highlight how the system can help them do more with less staff by automating manual tasks
  • Emphasize cost savings from reduced time-to-hire and improved quality of hires
  • Point out the scalable pricing model that grows with their business
  • Offer the 15-day free trial to prove value before commitment

For "Already Using Existing Tools" Objection:

1. Focus on integration and upgrade benefits:

  • Highlight the comprehensive feature set that replaces multiple disconnected tools
  • Emphasize the benefits of having everything in one unified platform vs fragmented systems
  • Point out modern features like AI sourcing and mobile apps that older systems may lack
  • Discuss easy data migration from existing systems to minimize disruption

Additional Selling Points:

  • No long-term contract required for self-service customers
  • Over 30,000 companies already trust and use the platform
  • Extensive integration capabilities with 70+ recruiting and HR tools
  • Dedicated support team and implementation assistance
  • Compliance features built-in (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)

The key is to position Workable as an investment in growth and efficiency rather than just another expense, while emphasizing how it provides significantly more value than their current tools.
