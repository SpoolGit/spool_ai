Objection Handling +

Based on the context and putting myself in the seller's shoes, here's how I would handle these objections:

For Price/Cost Objection:

  1. Emphasize ROI and efficiency gains:
    • Highlight how the software can save significant time by automating manual tasks
    • Point out that for a small team of 2, every hour saved on administrative work means more time for revenue-generating activities
    • Offer a 15-day free trial to demonstrate value without upfront commitment

For "Current Process Works Fine" Objection:

  1. Focus on growth and scalability:
    • Acknowledge their current process while highlighting limitations as they grow
    • Explain how the software can help them handle increased volume of candidates without adding headcount
    • Emphasize that implementing now will prevent growing pains later when they're busier

Additional tips for both objections:

  • Keep the focus on their specific industry needs (hospitality/recruitment staffing)
  • Start with basic features they need most rather than overwhelming with full functionality
  • Position it as an investment in their business's future rather than just an expense
  • Highlight the mobile-friendly features that allow them to manage hiring on-the-go

The key is to demonstrate clear value and ROI while acknowledging their current size and specific industry needs.
