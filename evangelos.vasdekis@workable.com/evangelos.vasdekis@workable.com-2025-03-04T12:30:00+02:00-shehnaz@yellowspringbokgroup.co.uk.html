Objection Handling +

Based on the context and my experience, here are effective responses to handle these common objections:

For Cost/Budget concerns:

  1. Focus on ROI and cost savings:
    • Highlight how the software can reduce agency fees and recruitment costs
    • Emphasize time savings through automation of manual tasks
    • Point to case studies like Alexander Dennis who saved $300k in agency fees in first year
  2. Demonstrate value vs. current spend:
    • Break down cost per hire with and without the system
    • Show how improved efficiency leads to faster time-to-hire (like Lethbridge's 50% reduction)
    • Explain how better candidate experience helps win top talent in competitive market

For "Current process works fine":

  1. Address pain points without criticizing:
    • Ask about their current recruitment challenges and bottlenecks
    • Discuss how automation can free up time for strategic work
    • Show how digital transformation improves collaboration between teams
  2. Focus on competitive advantage:
    • Highlight how modern candidates expect streamlined digital processes
    • Demonstrate features like automated sourcing that help find passive candidates
    • Emphasize ability to move quickly on good candidates before competitors

The key is to focus on business value and ROI rather than just features, and to position the solution as an enhancement to their current process rather than a replacement. Keep the conversation centered on their specific challenges and goals.
