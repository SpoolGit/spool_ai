Objection Handling +

Based on the context and my experience, here are the key arguments to overcome these objections:

For Cost/Price concerns:

  1. ROI-focused approach: Highlight how the platform can help them save money by:
    • Reducing time spent on manual recruitment tasks
    • Improving quality of hires through better screening
    • Minimizing costly bad hires through standardized evaluation
    • Scaling their recruitment efficiently as they grow
  2. Flexible pricing: Emphasize that:
    • They can start with core features and add more as needed
    • No long-term commitment required with monthly plans
    • Free trial available to test the value before committing
    • Price scales with their team size

For "Current system works fine":

  1. Growth & Scalability: Position it as an investment in their future by:
    • Having a system that can grow with them seamlessly
    • Building proper recruitment infrastructure early
    • Standardizing their hiring process before it becomes chaotic
    • Saving time now that can be spent on strategic initiatives
  2. Modern Candidate Experience:
    • Provide a professional, branded careers page
    • Mobile-friendly application process
    • Automated communications to keep candidates engaged
    • Integration with popular job boards and social media

The key is focusing on concrete business value and ROI rather than just features, while acknowledging their current size and offering flexible options that can scale with their growth.
