Objection Handling +

Based on the context and putting myself in the seller's shoes, here are concise responses to overcome these objections:

For Cost/Budget concerns:

  1. Emphasize ROI through automation - the platform automates administrative tasks, reducing manual work and saving staff time/costs
  2. Offer a 15-day free trial with no credit card required to demonstrate value before commitment
  3. Highlight how the system can "grow with you" - only pay for what you need initially
  4. Point out how better hiring decisions through standardized processes can reduce costly bad hires

For "Current system works fine":

  1. Acknowledge their existing process while highlighting pain points it may not address (e.g., manual tasks, lack of automation)
  2. Reference the testimonial showing how the platform provides clearer reporting and a "single source of truth" that executives trust
  3. Emphasize modern features their current system may lack (AI screening, self-scheduled interviews, automated approvals)
  4. Offer to do a side-by-side comparison of their current process vs. Workable's automation capabilities to demonstrate concrete improvements

The key is focusing on value and efficiency gains rather than just features, while making it easy for them to try the system risk-free.
