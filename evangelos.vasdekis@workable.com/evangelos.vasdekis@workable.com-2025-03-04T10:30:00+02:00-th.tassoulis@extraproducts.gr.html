    • Emphasize time savings through automation of manual tasks which frees up staff time
    • Point out that one successful hire through improved processes could pay for the system

For "Current system works fine" objection:

  1. Acknowledge that their current process may work, but highlight opportunities for improvement:
    • Faster hiring process through automation and better candidate management
    • Better collaboration between team members with shared access
    • Reduced risk of losing good candidates due to slow/manual processes
    • Future scalability as the company grows without needing to change systems again

The key is to:

  1. Focus on concrete business value rather than just features
  2. Position the software as an investment in growth and efficiency rather than just an expense
  3. Highlight how even small companies can benefit from professional hiring tools to compete for talent
  4. Emphasize that waiting to improve processes could mean missing out on great candidates

Keep the conversation focused on their specific pain points and challenges rather than generic product features.
