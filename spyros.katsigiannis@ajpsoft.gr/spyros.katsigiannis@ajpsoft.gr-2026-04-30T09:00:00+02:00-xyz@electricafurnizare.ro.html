Summary

Sales Cheatsheet: Sorin D., Electrica Furnizare

This sales cheatsheet targets Sorin D., Director of Network Management at Electrica Furnizare, a tech-literate leader navigating the operational fallout of a massive recent IT migration.

The company is currently struggling with billing inaccuracies, mass invoice reversals (stornos), and heavy manual workloads caused by complex Romanian energy price-capping regulations.

AJPSoft’s IEBS 4 platform is positioned as the solution to automate these regulatory shifts, reduce 'atypical' billing exceptions through pre-generation audits, and synchronize fragmented data between network operations and retail billing to restore customer trust and operational efficiency.

Current Situation Analysis

Electrica Furnizare

Electrica Furnizare (part of the Electrica Group) is the largest electricity supplier in Romania, managing a massive portfolio of over 3.5 million B2C and B2B customers across 18 counties. They provide both electricity and natural gas and act as a Supplier of Last Resort.

Recently (Feb 2024), the company completed a 'large-scale modernization' of its IT systems. While this won them industry awards (like the recent SAP innovation award), the transition caused severe operational disruptions:

  • Delayed invoices
  • Changed client IDs
  • Issuance of highly inaccurate 'atypical' bills resulting in mass invoice cancellations (storno)

Furthermore, they are under immense pressure to accurately apply complex government price caps (plafonare) and compensations while fending off high volumes of customer complaints regarding estimated consumption ('regularization') and payment allocation errors.

They are publicly listed (Bucharest/London) and highly scrutinized.

Identified Pain Points

Primary (High Relevance):

  • Inability of the new IT system to handle edge cases smoothly, resulting in billing inaccuracies, 'atypical' consumption values, and massive 'storno' (reversal) procedures.
  • Heavy operational friction caused by manual adjustments needed to comply with complex, evolving Romanian energy legislation (OUG 27/2022).
  • Fragmented data flow resulting in unregistered customer payments and delayed bill deliveries, spiking call center volumes.

Secondary (Moderate Relevance):

  • Customer dissatisfaction with high 'regularization' bills due to poor handling of estimated versus actual consumption data.
  • Disputes over natural gas billing specifically related to the calculation of the higher calorific value.
  • Exposure to wholesale market price shocks impacting overall profitability.
