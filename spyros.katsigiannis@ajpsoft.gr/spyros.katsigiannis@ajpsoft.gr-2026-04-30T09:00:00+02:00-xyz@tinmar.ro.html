Summary

Sales Cheatsheet: Neacsu Valentin-Razvan (Tinmar Energy)

This sales cheatsheet targets Neacsu Valentin-Razvan, the technical-minded IT Director at Tinmar Energy, Romania’s largest private energy provider. Tinmar is currently scaling from a B2B focus to an ambitious 3-million B2C household goal across 21 countries, which is straining their legacy architecture.

The strategy focuses on positioning AJPSoft’s IEBS 4, NGAS, and ETRM solutions as high-performance, API-first tools capable of handling:

  • Complex hybrid tariffs (energy + insurance)
  • Automated gas allocations
  • Centralized cross-border trading

To resonate with Neacsu’s software engineering background, the pitch should emphasize 'tariffs as software,' architectural scalability for the 'MyTin' digital ecosystem, and the reduction of manual IT bottlenecks caused by regulatory changes and massive data volume.

Qualification Questions

1. Architecture & Scalability (B2C Expansion):

  • 'As you scale towards your ambitious goal of 3 million residential customers, where is your current billing architecture experiencing the most friction—calculation speed, or the validation and exception reviews?'

2. System Interconnection (APIs):

  • 'How seamlessly does your current core billing system feed data into customer-facing platforms like the MyTin app and MyAccount portal? Are there manual syncs or latency issues?'

3. Complex Tariff Management:

  • 'With the introduction of complex packages (Gold, Silver) that include non-energy services like appliance insurance, how much custom IT intervention is required to configure and bill these hybrid tariffs?'

4. Wholesale Market & Risk (ETRM):

  • 'Tinmar is trading across 21 countries and multiple platforms (OPCOM, HUPX, EEX). How are you currently centralizing transaction management and hedging risk across these fragmented markets?'

5. Natural Gas Specifics (NGAS):

  • 'For your growing Tinmar Gas division, how automated is your consumption profiling and correction factor application (e.g., Higher Calorific Power)?'
Pre-Call Hypotheses

Hypothesis 1: The B2B to B2C Transition is Straining Legacy Systems:

Tinmar's systems were likely built for high-volume, low-count industrial clients (B2B). Expanding to 3 million B2C households is likely causing massive validation bottlenecks and exception handling for operations and IT.

Hypothesis 2: Siloed Data Across Divisions:

With aggressive growth across Tinmar Energy, Gas, and Oil, plus wholesale trading across 21 countries, it's highly probable that data is fragmented. The IT team is likely spending too much time building custom ETL pipelines rather than innovating.

Hypothesis 3: 'Tariffs as Software' Nightmare:

Managing customized bundles (insurance + energy) and handling smart home/BESS meter data is forcing Tinmar's IT team to hardcode billing logic, making the system rigid and difficult to update when Romanian or EU regulations change.

Hypothesis 4: Manual Interventions in Gas:

With Tinmar Gas growing rapidly from 70 to 1,000+ major clients, the allocation management and correction factor calculations are likely highly manual or managed in sophisticated but fragile spreadsheets.

Strategic Drivers

1. Massive B2C Market Capture:

Achieving their goal of 3 million residential households requires extreme operational efficiency and automated, error-free billing at scale.

2. International Expansion:

Licensing and operating in new markets (Croatia, Poland, Slovenia) requires a core system that can quickly adapt to new regional grid operators and regulatory frameworks.

3. Digital-First Customer Experience:

Reducing churn and winning customers via seamless digital onboarding, smart home integration, and transparent billing visible on the MyTin app.

4. Asset Value Creation (Renewables):

Maximizing ROI on their 23+ photovoltaic parks and BESS (Battery Energy Storage Systems) by efficiently trading the generated power and managing green certificates.
