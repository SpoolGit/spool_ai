Objection Handling +

Based on the context and my expertise as a sales researcher, here's how I would handle these objections:

1. Cost/Budget concerns:

  • Focus on ROI and cost savings: Highlight that Workable can reduce time-to-hire by up to 40 days and improve HR productivity by 30% according to their data
  • Emphasize cost elimination: Point out that Workable can help remove 100% of agency/third-party tool costs by consolidating recruiting functions into one platform
  • Demonstrate value: The platform includes 200+ job board integrations, automated scheduling, and AI-powered sourcing - getting multiple tools' worth of functionality in one solution

2. Current system resistance:

  • Highlight easy transition: Workable offers rapid system setup and data migration from existing systems to ensure continuity
  • Focus on modern capabilities: Emphasize AI-powered features, mobile apps, and integrations with modern tools like Zoom/Teams that their legacy system may lack
  • Reduce risk: Point out the 15-day free trial with no credit card required, allowing them to validate the benefits before fully committing
  • Compliance advantage: Stress automated compliance with GDPR, EEO/OFCCP & CCPA regulations, which may be manual work in their current system

The key is focusing on concrete business value and risk reduction rather than just features, while making the transition process seem manageable.
