Objection Handling +

Based on the context and my expertise, here are effective responses to handle these common sales objections:

1. Cost/Budget concerns - "The software is too expensive for our current budget"

Key arguments to overcome this:

  • Focus on ROI and cost savings: "While there is an upfront investment, the software helps reduce hiring costs through automation and efficiency gains"
  • Highlight value vs cost: "The platform offers comprehensive features including sourcing, ATS, onboarding and HR management - providing more value than standalone solutions"
  • Emphasize trial option: "We offer a 15-day free trial so you can evaluate the full platform and see the value firsthand before committing"
  • Discuss flexible pricing: "We have different pricing tiers that can align with your budget and scale as your needs grow"

2. Already have existing solutions/tools - "We already have recruitment tools/HR software that works for us"

Key arguments to overcome this:

  • Focus on integration & consolidation: "Workable can integrate with your existing tools while providing a single unified platform for all recruitment and HR needs"
  • Highlight modern features: "Our platform includes modern capabilities like AI-powered screening, automated sourcing, and advanced analytics that may be missing from legacy systems"
  • Emphasize efficiency gains: "Having everything in one platform versus multiple tools can significantly improve team productivity and reduce administrative overhead"
  • Point out limitations: "While your current solution may work, are there gaps or manual processes that could be automated to save time and improve results?"

The key is to focus on value proposition and ROI rather than just features, while demonstrating understanding of the prospect's concerns.
