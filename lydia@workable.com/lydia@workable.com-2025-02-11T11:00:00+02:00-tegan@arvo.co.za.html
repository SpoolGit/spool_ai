Objection Handling +

Based on the context and putting myself in the seller's shoes, here are the key arguments to overcome these objections:

For Cost/Budget concerns:

  1. Highlight the ROI through time savings and automation - The software automates repetitive tasks, emails, and scheduling, allowing the small team to focus on higher-value activities
  2. Offer flexible pricing options including a 15-day free trial with no credit card required to test the full platform
  3. Emphasize how the platform can scale with their growth, so they only pay for what they need now

For "Current process works fine":

  1. Point out efficiency gains through features like:
    • One-click job posting to 200+ sites
    • Self-scheduling tools that eliminate back-and-forth emails
    • Automated candidate communications
  2. Highlight how even small teams can benefit from structured hiring processes and evaluation tools to make better hiring decisions
  3. Demonstrate how the platform can help them build a talent pool for future needs by resurfacing past candidates and managing referrals

The key is to focus on immediate practical benefits that address their specific pain points as a small staffing firm, rather than overwhelming them with all features. Emphasize how the software can help them do more with their limited resources.
