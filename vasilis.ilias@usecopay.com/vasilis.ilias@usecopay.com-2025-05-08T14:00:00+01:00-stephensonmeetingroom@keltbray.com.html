Short Description:

Keltbray is a prominent UK engineering and construction services company, established in 1976. It is privately owned and has grown significantly under the leadership of Brendan Kerr, who became the sole owner and CEO in 2003. With over 1,700 employees, Keltbray is known for its self-delivery capability across various phases of the construction value chain.

The company offers a diverse range of services, including civil engineering, geotechnical services, asbestos management, demolition, piling, reinforced concrete structures, decommissioning, remediation, rail services, power distribution, and environmental services. Keltbray focuses on sustainable practices and has received the RoSPA Gold Award for safety for nine consecutive years. The company is committed to delivering projects safely and efficiently, contributing to the development and maintenance of the UK's economic infrastructure.

Handling Objection
Based on the context and putting myself in the seller's shoes, here are the most relevant counter-arguments for each objection:

1. For "We already have existing financing/factoring arrangements in place":

  • Unlike traditional factoring, this is a no-debt solution that works directly with your clients
  • The solution is more cost-effective than factoring, which is "expensive and hard to stop using once you start" (as quoted from an MD testimonial)
  • You maintain direct relationships with your clients rather than involving third-party finance companies
  • You can use it flexibly alongside existing arrangements - it's not an all-or-nothing solution

2. For "Our current payment processes and systems work fine - why change them?":

  • The system integrates with existing platforms (like COINS) with minimal disruption
  • It creates a win-win situation where both you and your clients benefit financially
  • You get real-time visibility of invoice/payment data through a free 24/7 portal
  • It's not just about fixing problems - it's about improving cash flow efficiency and generating additional value
  • You maintain control by choosing when to accelerate payments based on your needs

The key is to position this as an enhancement to their current processes rather than a replacement, emphasizing the flexibility and additional benefits they don't currently have.
