  2. Do you have experience in the car rental or automotive industry, and if so, what has been your focus (e.g., sales, fleet management, customer service)?

    - This gauges the prospect’s interest in leveraging Surprice’s established brand and support systems.
  5. What is your current customer base, and how do you plan to integrate Surprice’s online booking facilities and competitive rates into your existing operations?
    - This evaluates the prospect’s ability to integrate Surprice’s B2B solutions and expand their customer reach.

These questions are designed to assess the prospect’s alignment with Surprice’s offerings, their readiness for a partnership or franchise, and their ability to benefit from Surprice’s resources and expertise.

Objection Handling +

Here are the most relevant arguments the seller can use to overcome the objections of "Cost concerns" and "Lack of trust in the brand" :

1. Cost Concerns

  • Value for Money : Highlight the competitive pricing and the value customers receive, such as 24/7 service, multiple insurance options, and a wide range of car types.
  • Special Offers : Mention ongoing promotions or discounts that make the service more affordable.
  • Low-Cost Operations : Emphasize that the company’s efficient operations allow for lower costs, which are passed on to customers.

2. Lack of Trust in the Brand

  • Awards and Recognition : Reference the Gold Award for Franchise Business Model Innovation 2021 to establish credibility and innovation.
  • Global Presence : Highlight the extensive network across multiple countries, showcasing reliability and experience.
  • Customer Support : Stress the 24/7 customer service and professional team dedicated to ensuring customer satisfaction.
  • Social Proof : Point to active social media presence (Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, etc.) and positive customer interactions.

These arguments address the objections directly by focusing on affordability, value, and trust-building elements.
