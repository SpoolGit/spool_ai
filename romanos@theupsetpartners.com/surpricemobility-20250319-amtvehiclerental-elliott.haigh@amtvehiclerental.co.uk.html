Objection Handling +

Here are the most relevant arguments the seller can use to overcome the objections of "Cost concerns" and "Lack of trust in the brand" :

1. Cost Concerns

  • Value for Money : Highlight the competitive pricing and the value provided, such as 24/7 service, multiple insurance options, and a wide range of car types.
  • Special Offers : Mention ongoing promotions or discounts that make the service more affordable.
  • Cost Efficiency : Emphasize low operational costs and competitive rates compared to other brands.
  • Long-Term Savings : Explain how the service can save money in the long run through reliable and efficient operations.

2. Lack of Trust in the Brand

  • Global Presence : Showcase the brand’s expansion into multiple countries (e.g., Canada, Italy, Greece) and its growing reputation.
  • Customer Testimonials : Share positive reviews or awards (e.g., recognition in Montenegro and Serbia) to build credibility.
  • Professional Support : Highlight the expertise of the team, including training programs and 24/7 support.
  • Transparency : Point to clear policies (e.g., privacy, cookie, and terms of use) and a dedicated help center for customer inquiries.
  • Partnerships : Mention collaborations with global brokers and travel agencies, reinforcing trust in the brand’s reliability.

By addressing these objections with specific, evidence-based arguments, the seller can effectively reassure potential customers.
