Qualification Questions +

Based on the provided context and the seller's business offering (franchise opportunities and partnerships with existing car rental companies), here are the 5 most relevant and important questions to ask the prospect:

  1. What is your current business model, and are you looking to expand or enhance your car rental operations?

  2. Do you have experience in the car rental industry, and what challenges are you currently facing?
    - Understanding their experience and pain points helps tailor the Surprice solution to their needs.
  3. Are you interested in leveraging a global sales network and established brand to increase your market presence?
    - This highlights Surprice's value proposition of providing a global network and brand recognition.
  4. What is your current IT infrastructure, and would you benefit from access to Surprice's IT support and online booking facilities?
    - This assesses their technical needs and whether Surprice's IT solutions would be a good fit.
  5. Are you open to a franchise or partnership model that includes training, operational support, and competitive rates?
    - This directly addresses Surprice's core offerings and gauges the prospect's interest in a structured partnership.

These questions are designed to qualify the prospect's fit with Surprice's business model and identify opportunities for collaboration.

Objection Handling +

Here are the most relevant arguments the seller can use to overcome the objections:

  1. Cost Concerns:

    • Highlight the value-for-money proposition, emphasizing that the service offers competitive rates while maintaining high-quality standards.
    • Mention special offers and discounts that make the service more affordable.
    • Explain the long-term benefits of choosing a reliable service, such as 24/7 support and low-cost operations, which can save money in the long run.

  2. Lack of Trust in the Brand:

    • Reference the WTA Gold Winner award for Greece’s leading car rental company in 2021, showcasing industry recognition and credibility.
    • Emphasize the global reach and franchise network , which demonstrate the brand’s reliability and widespread trust.
    • Highlight the 24/7 customer support and fully trained team of experts , ensuring a professional and trustworthy experience.
    • Share positive customer testimonials or case studies to build confidence in the brand.

These arguments address the objections directly by focusing on affordability, value, and trustworthiness.
