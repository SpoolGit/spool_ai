Qualification Questions +

Based on the provided context, here are the 5 most relevant and important questions the seller (Surprice Car Rentals) should ask the prospect (HSMAI Europe) to determine if they are a good fit for their B2B offering, which includes franchise opportunities and partnerships with existing car rental companies:

  1. What is your current involvement in the hospitality industry, and how do you see car rental services complementing your existing offerings?
    (This helps assess if the prospect’s business aligns with Surprice’s services and if there’s a natural synergy.)
  2. Are you currently working with or considering partnerships with car rental companies to enhance your clients' travel and hospitality experiences?
    (This identifies if the prospect is already exploring or open to partnerships in the car rental space.)
  3. What are your goals for revenue optimization and customer satisfaction in the hotel, event, and travel sectors, and how could a car rental partnership contribute to these goals?
    (This helps understand the prospect’s priorities and how Surprice’s services could add value.)
  4. Do you have an existing network or client base that could benefit from integrated car rental services, such as online booking facilities or competitive rates?
    (This evaluates the prospect’s potential to leverage Surprice’s B2B solutions for their clients.)
  5. Are you interested in exploring franchise opportunities to expand your service offerings under a globally recognized car rental brand?
    (This directly assesses the prospect’s interest in Surprice’s franchise model and their willingness to expand their business.)

These questions are designed to uncover the prospect’s needs, goals, and alignment with Surprice Car Rentals’ offerings, ensuring a mutually beneficial partnership.

Objection Handling +

Here are the most relevant arguments the seller can use to overcome the objections of "Cost concerns" and "Lack of trust in the brand" :

1. Cost Concerns

  • Value for Money : Highlight the competitive pricing and the value customers receive, such as 24/7 service, multiple insurance options, and a wide range of car types.
  • Special Offers : Mention ongoing promotions or discounts that make the service more affordable.
  • Cost Efficiency : Emphasize the low operational costs and competitive rates compared to other brands.
  • Long-Term Savings : Explain how the quality of service and reliability can save customers money in the long run by avoiding hidden fees or poor service from competitors.

2. Lack of Trust in the Brand

  • Global Presence : Showcase the brand’s extensive network across multiple countries and its growing reputation.
  • Customer Support : Highlight the 24/7 customer service and dedicated support team to build confidence in the brand’s reliability.
  • Testimonials and Awards : Use positive customer reviews, awards, and recognitions (e.g., awards in Montenegro and Serbia) to establish credibility.
  • Transparency : Point to the clear terms and conditions, privacy policy, and cookie policy as evidence of the brand’s commitment to transparency and customer trust.
  • Franchise Success : Mention the success of franchise partners and the brand’s support system (training, IT infrastructure, and global web presence) as proof of its reliability and professionalism.

By addressing these objections with specific, evidence-based arguments, the seller can effectively reassure potential customers.
