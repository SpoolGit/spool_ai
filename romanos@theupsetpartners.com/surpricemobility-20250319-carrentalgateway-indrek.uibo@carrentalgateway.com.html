Qualification Questions +

Based on the provided context, here are the 5 most relevant and important questions the seller should ask the prospect to determine if they are a good fit for Surprice Car Rentals' B2B offering, which includes franchise opportunities and partnerships with existing car rental companies:

  1. What is your current experience in the car rental or mobility industry?
    - This helps assess the prospect's familiarity with the industry and their ability to manage a franchise or partnership effectively.
  2. Do you have an existing car rental business, or are you looking to start one?
    - This clarifies whether the prospect is seeking to expand their current operations or enter the car rental market for the first time.
  3. What is your target market or geographic location for this business?
    - This helps determine if the prospect's market aligns with Surprice's expansion goals and global sales network.
  4. What are your expectations regarding training, IT infrastructure, and ongoing support?
    - This ensures the prospect understands and values the resources Surprice provides to franchisees and partners.
  5. What are your financial goals and investment capacity for this opportunity?
    - This helps evaluate the prospect's readiness to invest in a franchise or partnership and their alignment with Surprice's value-for-money proposition.

These questions are designed to assess the prospect's fit with Surprice's business model, their ability to leverage the provided resources, and their potential for long-term success as a franchisee or partner.

Objection Handling +

Here are the most relevant arguments the seller can use to overcome the objections:

Cost Concerns

  1. Value for Money : Emphasize the competitive pricing and the quality of service provided, ensuring customers get the best value for their money.
  2. Special Offers : Highlight any ongoing promotions, discounts, or flexible pricing options that make the service more affordable.
  3. Long-Term Savings : Explain how the service can save costs in the long run, such as through efficient operations or bundled services.

Lack of Trust in the Brand

  1. Global Presence : Mention the brand’s extensive network and presence in multiple countries, showcasing its reliability and reputation.
  2. Customer Support : Highlight the 24/7 customer service and support available, ensuring customers feel secure and assisted at all times.
  3. Testimonials and Reviews : Share positive feedback, awards, or recognitions the brand has received to build credibility.
  4. Transparency : Point out the clear terms and conditions, privacy policies, and customer-focused practices that demonstrate trustworthiness.

These arguments address the objections directly and leverage the brand’s strengths to reassure potential customers.
