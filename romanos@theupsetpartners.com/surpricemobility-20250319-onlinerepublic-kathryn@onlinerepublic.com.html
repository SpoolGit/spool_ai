Objection Handling +

Here are the most relevant arguments the seller can use to overcome the objections:

  1. Cost Concerns

    • Highlight the value-for-money proposition, emphasizing competitive rates and low-cost operations.
    • Mention special offers and discounts tailored to meet market needs.
    • Explain the long-term benefits of the service, such as quality, reliability, and 24/7 support, which justify the cost.

  2. Lack of Trust in the Brand

    • Showcase the brand’s global reach and presence in multiple countries, emphasizing its growing reputation.
    • Provide testimonials or case studies from satisfied customers or franchise partners.
    • Highlight the training and support provided to franchisees, ensuring a high level of service and professionalism.
    • Point to the brand’s online presence (e.g., social media, website) and positive reviews to build credibility.

If the context doesn’t directly address these objections, the seller can focus on transparency , customer satisfaction , and proven results to build trust and justify the cost.
