Qualification Questions +

Based on the provided context, here are the 5 most relevant and important questions the seller should ask the prospect to determine if they are a good fit for Surprice Car Rentals' B2B offering (franchise opportunities and partnerships with existing car rental companies):

  1. What is your current experience in the car rental or motor vehicle industry?
    - This helps assess the prospect's familiarity with the industry and their ability to manage a car rental business under the Surprice brand.
  2. Do you have an existing car rental business or are you looking to start one?
    - This clarifies whether the prospect is seeking a partnership to expand their current operations or to establish a new franchise.
  3. What is your target market or region for this business?
    - Understanding the prospect's geographic focus helps determine if it aligns with Surprice's expansion goals and market strategy.
  4. What are your expectations regarding training, support, and IT infrastructure?
    - This ensures the prospect understands and values the comprehensive support Surprice provides, including training, IT systems, and operational guidance.
  5. What are your financial goals and investment capacity for this venture?
    - This helps gauge the prospect's financial readiness and commitment to the franchise or partnership model.

These questions are designed to assess the prospect's alignment with Surprice's business model, their operational readiness, and their ability to leverage the brand's resources effectively.

Objection Handling +

Here are the most relevant arguments the seller can use to overcome the objections of "Cost concerns" and "Lack of trust in the brand" :

1. Cost Concerns

  • Value for Money : Emphasize that the service offers optimal prices while maintaining high-quality standards, ensuring customers get the best value for their money.
  • Special Offers : Highlight ongoing special offers and discounts tailored to meet market and customer needs.
  • 24/7 Service : Stress the availability of round-the-clock support, which adds convenience and reduces potential additional costs.
  • Insurance Options : Mention the availability of multiple insurance schemes, providing flexibility and cost-effectiveness for different budgets.

2. Lack of Trust in the Brand

  • Global Reach : Highlight the brand’s extensive network across multiple countries, showcasing its reliability and experience.
  • Customer Testimonials : Share positive feedback or testimonials from satisfied customers to build credibility.
  • Expertise : Emphasize the team’s skill and passion for delivering quality service, ensuring customer satisfaction.
  • Transparency : Point to the brand’s clear policies (e.g., privacy, cookie, and terms of use) as evidence of its commitment to transparency and professionalism.
  • Social Proof : Reference the brand’s active presence on social media platforms (Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube) to demonstrate its engagement and trustworthiness.

If the context doesn’t provide enough information, these arguments can still effectively address the objections.
