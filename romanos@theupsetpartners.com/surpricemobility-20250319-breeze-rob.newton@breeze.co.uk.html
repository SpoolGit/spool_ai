Qualification Questions +

Based on the provided context, here are the 5 most relevant and important questions the seller (Surprice Car Rentals) should ask the prospect (Breeze Motor Group) to determine if they are a good fit for the B2B offering of franchise opportunities and partnerships:

  1. What is your current business model, and how do you see a partnership or franchise opportunity with Surprice Car Rentals fitting into your operations?
    - This helps understand if the prospect’s business aligns with Surprice’s franchise and partnership model.
  2. Do you have experience in the car rental or automotive retail industry, and if so, what specific services or brands have you worked with?
    - This assesses the prospect’s industry expertise and whether they can leverage Surprice’s offerings effectively.
  3. What are your goals for expanding your business, and how do you envision leveraging Surprice’s global sales network and IT infrastructure to achieve them?
    - This evaluates the prospect’s growth ambitions and their ability to utilize Surprice’s resources.
  4. Are you currently offering fleet solutions or vehicle rentals, and if so, what challenges are you facing that Surprice could help address?
    - This identifies pain points and opportunities for Surprice to provide value.
  5. What is your geographic presence, and are you interested in expanding into new markets with the support of Surprice’s established brand and operational expertise?
    - This determines if the prospect’s location and expansion plans align with Surprice’s global network.

These questions are designed to assess the prospect’s fit with Surprice’s B2B offerings, their ability to leverage Surprice’s resources, and their potential for long-term partnership success.

Objection Handling +

Here are the most relevant arguments the seller can use to overcome the objections:

  1. Cost Concerns:

    • Highlight the value-for-money proposition, emphasizing affordable rates and flexible terms.
    • Mention no hidden charges or fees , ensuring transparency in pricing.
    • Offer special deals or discounts for first-time customers or long-term rentals.
    • Stress the quality of service and well-maintained cars , ensuring customers get the best value for their money.

  2. Lack of Trust in the Brand:

    • Showcase positive customer testimonials to build credibility and trust.

    • Emphasize the global reach and network of franchises, indicating reliability and scalability.
    • Mention the quality insurance service and fully documented ICT operations, ensuring safety and transparency.

These arguments address the objections directly by focusing on affordability, transparency, and trust-building elements.
