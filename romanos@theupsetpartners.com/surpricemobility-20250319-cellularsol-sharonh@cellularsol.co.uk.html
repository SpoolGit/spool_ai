Qualification Questions +

Based on the provided context, here are the 5 most relevant and important questions the seller should ask the prospect to determine if they are a good fit for Surprice Car Rentals' B2B offering, which includes franchise opportunities and partnerships with existing car rental companies:

  1. What is your current business model, and do you operate in the car rental or automotive sector?
    - This helps determine if the prospect’s industry aligns with Surprice’s focus on car rental services and franchise opportunities.
  2. Do you have experience in managing or operating a car rental business, or are you looking to enter this market?
    - This question assesses the prospect’s expertise and whether they can leverage Surprice’s training and support effectively.
  3. Are you interested in expanding your operations internationally, and do you operate in any of the countries where Surprice is active (e.g., Australia, Germany, France, etc.)?
    - This identifies if the prospect’s geographic presence aligns with Surprice’s global network.
  4. What are your goals for growth, and how do you see a partnership with Surprice helping you achieve them?
    - This helps understand the prospect’s objectives and whether they align with Surprice’s value proposition of global sales networks, IT infrastructure, and brand support.
  5. Are you looking for a franchise opportunity or a partnership to enhance your existing car rental operations?
    - This clarifies the prospect’s needs and whether they are interested in Surprice’s franchise model or B2B solutions for travel agencies and tourism businesses.

These questions are designed to assess the prospect’s fit with Surprice’s offerings and ensure alignment in goals, expertise, and geographic presence.

Objection Handling +

Here are the most relevant arguments the seller can use to overcome the objections of "Cost concerns" and "Lack of trust in the brand" :

1. Cost Concerns

  • Value for Money : Emphasize the quality, reliability, and additional benefits (e.g., 24/7 service, extensive support) that justify the cost.
  • Competitive Pricing : Highlight any competitive pricing or special offers, discounts, or packages that make the service more affordable.
  • Long-Term Savings : Explain how the product or service can save money in the long run (e.g., durability, efficiency, or reduced maintenance costs).

2. Lack of Trust in the Brand

  • Proven Track Record : Share testimonials, reviews, or case studies from satisfied customers to build credibility.
  • Transparency : Highlight the company’s clear terms and conditions, privacy policy, and customer support availability (e.g., 24/7 help center).
  • Social Proof : Point to the brand’s presence on social media platforms (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn) and its active engagement with customers.
  • Guarantees : Offer guarantees, such as a satisfaction guarantee or a money-back policy, to reduce perceived risk.

If the context doesn’t provide specific details, these general strategies can effectively address the objections.
