Qualification Questions +

Based on the provided context, here are the 5 most relevant and important questions the seller (Surprice Car Rentals) should ask the prospect (a company in the retail motor vehicles industry) to determine if they are a good fit for a franchise or partnership opportunity:

  1. What is your current business model, and how do you see a partnership with Surprice Car Rentals complementing or expanding your operations?
    - This helps assess alignment between the prospect's business and Surprice's franchise/partnership offerings.
  2. Do you have experience in the car rental industry, and if so, what has been your approach to customer service and fleet management?
    - Surprice emphasizes high-quality services and training, so understanding the prospect's experience is crucial.
  3. What are your goals for growth in the next 3-5 years, and how do you envision leveraging Surprice’s global sales network and IT infrastructure to achieve them?
    - This question evaluates the prospect's ambition and how well they can utilize Surprice's resources.
  4. Are you open to adopting Surprice’s branding, operational guidelines, and training programs to ensure consistency across the network?
    - Surprice provides extensive training and branding support, so the prospect must be willing to align with these standards.
  5. What is your current customer base, and how do you plan to integrate Surprice’s services to enhance their experience and expand your market reach?
    - This helps determine if the prospect can effectively integrate Surprice’s offerings to grow their business and meet customer expectations.

These questions are designed to assess the prospect's readiness, alignment with Surprice’s values, and potential for a successful partnership.

Objection Handling +

Here are the most relevant arguments the seller can use to overcome the objections:

  1. Cost Concerns

    • Highlight the value-for-money proposition, emphasizing competitive rates and low-cost operations.
    • Mention special offers and discounts that make the service more affordable.
    • Explain the long-term benefits of the service, such as quality, reliability, and 24/7 support, which justify the cost.

  2. Lack of Trust in the Brand

    • Reference the WTA Gold Winner award for Greece’s leading car rental company, showcasing industry recognition and credibility.
    • Emphasize the global reach and franchise network , demonstrating the brand’s reliability and widespread acceptance.
    • Highlight customer testimonials , 24/7 support , and transparent policies (e.g., privacy, terms of use) to build trust.

These arguments address the objections directly by focusing on affordability, value, and trustworthiness.
