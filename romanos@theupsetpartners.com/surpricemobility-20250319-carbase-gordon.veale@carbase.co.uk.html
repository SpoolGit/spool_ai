Qualification Questions +

Based on the provided context, here are the 5 most relevant and important questions the seller (Surprice Car Rentals) should ask the prospect (a company in the Retail Motor Vehicles industry with over 200 staff members in the UK) to determine if they are a good fit for franchise opportunities or partnerships:

  1. What is your current business model, and do you have experience in car rental operations?
    - This helps assess if the prospect has the necessary expertise to operate a car rental franchise or partnership.
  2. Are you looking to expand your operations or enter new markets under an established brand?
    - This determines if the prospect is interested in leveraging Surprice’s global brand and network for growth.
  3. What is your current fleet size, and how do you manage fleet operations?
    - This helps evaluate the prospect’s existing infrastructure and whether they can integrate with Surprice’s systems.
  4. Are you interested in leveraging Surprice’s IT infrastructure, training programs, and global sales network?
    - This identifies if the prospect values the support and resources Surprice offers to franchisees and partners.
  5. What are your goals for profitability and market competitiveness, and how do you see Surprice helping you achieve them?
    - This assesses the prospect’s alignment with Surprice’s value proposition of low-cost operations and competitive rates.

These questions are designed to qualify the prospect’s readiness, alignment with Surprice’s offerings, and potential for a successful partnership.

Objection Handling +

Here are the most relevant arguments the seller can use to overcome the objections of "Cost concerns" and "Lack of trust in the brand" :

Cost Concerns

  1. Value for Money : Highlight the competitive pricing and the value-added services (e.g., 24/7 support, multiple insurance schemes, and global reach) that justify the cost.
  2. Special Offers : Mention ongoing promotions or discounts that make the service more affordable.
  3. Long-Term Savings : Emphasize how the service can save money in the long run through efficient operations and low-cost management.

Lack of Trust in the Brand

  1. Global Presence : Point out the extensive network of franchises and operations in multiple countries, showcasing the brand’s reliability and reach.
  2. Customer Testimonials : Share positive reviews or case studies from satisfied customers to build credibility.
  3. Transparency : Highlight the company’s clear terms and conditions, privacy policy, and customer support channels to demonstrate trustworthiness.

If the context doesn’t provide enough information, these arguments are still effective for addressing the objections.
