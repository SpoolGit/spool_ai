    (This gauges the prospect's expectations and readiness to leverage Surprice’s training and support systems.)

These questions are designed to assess the prospect's fit with Surprice’s business model, their operational capabilities, and their alignment with the company’s goals and resources.

Objection Handling +

Here are the most relevant arguments the seller can use to overcome the objections of "Cost concerns" and "Lack of trust in the brand" :

1. Cost Concerns

  • Value for Money : Highlight the competitive pricing and the value-added services (e.g., 24/7 customer support, multiple insurance options, and global reach) that justify the cost.
  • Special Offers : Mention ongoing promotions, discounts, or flexible pricing plans to make the service more affordable.
  • Long-Term Savings : Emphasize how the service can save money in the long run (e.g., low-cost operations, efficient fleet management).

2. Lack of Trust in the Brand

  • Proven Track Record : Reference awards like the "Franchise Business Model Innovation 2021" and the company’s 12+ years of experience in the car rental industry.
  • Global Presence : Highlight the extensive network of franchises and operations in multiple countries, showcasing reliability and scalability.
  • Customer Support : Stress the 24/7 customer service and the availability of a dedicated help center to address any concerns.
  • Testimonials and Reviews : Share positive feedback from existing customers or franchisees to build credibility.

These arguments address the objections directly and leverage the company’s strengths to build confidence and justify the cost.
