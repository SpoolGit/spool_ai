Qualification Questions +

Based on the provided context, here are the 5 most relevant and important questions the seller (Surprice Car Rentals) should ask the prospect (a retail motor vehicles company dealing with BMW, MINI, and Motorrad vehicles in Devon and Cornwall, UK) to determine if they are a good fit for their B2B offering of franchise opportunities and partnerships:

  1. Are you interested in expanding your business operations by offering car rental services under a globally recognized brand?
    (This helps gauge the prospect's interest in diversifying their services and leveraging Surprice's brand.)
  2. Do you have experience or interest in managing a franchise or partnership model within the automotive industry?
    (This assesses the prospect's familiarity with franchise operations and their readiness to adopt such a model.)
  3. What is your current customer base, and do you see potential in offering car rental services to them?
    (This evaluates the prospect's existing market and the potential for integrating car rental services.)
  4. Are you looking for a partner that provides comprehensive support, including IT infrastructure, training, and global sales network access?
    (This determines if the prospect values the operational and technical support Surprice offers.)
  5. What are your long-term business goals, and how do you see a partnership with Surprice Car Rentals helping you achieve them?
    (This helps align the prospect's objectives with Surprice's offerings and ensures mutual benefit.)

These questions are designed to assess the prospect's alignment with Surprice's franchise and partnership model, their readiness to adopt new services, and their long-term business goals.

Objection Handling +

Here are the most relevant arguments the seller can use to overcome the objections of "Cost concerns" and "Lack of trust in the brand" :

1. Cost Concerns

  • Value for Money : Highlight the competitive pricing and the value-added services (e.g., 24/7 support, extensive network, and quality vehicles) that justify the cost.
  • Cost Savings : Emphasize any discounts, promotions, or long-term savings (e.g., lower maintenance costs or fuel efficiency of vehicles).
  • Flexible Options : Offer flexible rental plans or packages tailored to the customer’s budget.
  • Transparency : Clearly explain the pricing structure to avoid hidden fees, building trust and reducing perceived cost concerns.

2. Lack of Trust in the Brand

  • Reputation and Awards : Mention the brand’s recognition, such as winning the World Travel Awards Gold Winner for Greece’s Leading Car Rental Company 2021 , to establish credibility.
  • Customer Testimonials : Share positive reviews or testimonials from satisfied customers to build trust.
  • Global Presence : Highlight the brand’s extensive network and partnerships worldwide, showcasing reliability and experience.
  • Security and Support : Emphasize the 24/7 customer support, secure booking process, and transparent policies to reassure customers.

If the context doesn’t provide specific details, these general strategies can effectively address the objections.
