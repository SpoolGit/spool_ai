Qualification Questions +

Based on the provided context and the seller's business offering (franchise opportunities and partnerships with existing car rental companies), here are the 5 most relevant and important questions to ask the prospect:

  1. What is your current business model, and how do you see a partnership or franchise opportunity with Surprice Car Rentals fitting into your operations?
    - This helps understand the prospect's business structure and alignment with Surprice's offerings.
  2. What are your primary goals for expanding or improving your car rental services, and how do you believe Surprice can help you achieve them?
    - This identifies the prospect's needs and how Surprice's global network, training, and IT infrastructure can add value.
  3. Do you have experience in the car rental industry, and if so, what challenges have you faced in scaling or maintaining operations?
    - This assesses the prospect's experience and highlights areas where Surprice's support can address their pain points.
  4. What is your current customer base, and how do you plan to leverage Surprice's brand and global sales network to attract more customers?
    - This evaluates the prospect's market reach and their potential to benefit from Surprice's established brand.
  5. What are your expectations regarding training, support, and operational costs, and how do you see Surprice's low-cost operations and competitive rates impacting your business?
    - This clarifies the prospect's expectations and ensures alignment with Surprice's value proposition.

These questions are designed to qualify the prospect's fit with Surprice's offerings and identify opportunities for a mutually beneficial partnership.

Objection Handling +

Here are the most relevant arguments the seller can use to overcome the objections:

1. Cost Concerns

  • Value for Money : Emphasize the quality of service, optimal pricing, and the value customers receive compared to competitors.
  • Special Offers : Highlight any ongoing promotions, discounts, or flexible payment options to make the cost more manageable.
  • Long-Term Savings : Explain how the service or product can save money in the long run (e.g., durability, efficiency, or reduced maintenance costs).

2. Lack of Trust in the Brand

  • Reputation and Reach : Mention the global presence and positive reputation of the brand, supported by customer testimonials or reviews.
  • 24/7 Support : Highlight the availability of 24/7 customer service and a dedicated help center to address any concerns.
  • Transparency : Reassure the customer with clear terms, privacy policies, and a commitment to quality and security.

If the context doesn’t provide enough information, these general strategies should effectively address the objections.
