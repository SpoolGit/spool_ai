    (This assesses the prospect's customer focus and their ability to integrate Surprice's B2B solutions.)

These questions are designed to evaluate the prospect's fit with Surprice Car Rentals' offerings, their readiness to adopt the brand's standards, and their potential for successful collaboration.

Objection Handling +

Here are the most relevant arguments the seller can use to overcome the objections of "Cost concerns" and "Lack of trust in the brand" :

1. Cost Concerns

  • Value for Money : Highlight the competitive pricing and the value-added services (e.g., 24/7 support, multiple insurance schemes, and optional extras) that make the cost worthwhile.
  • Special Offers : Mention ongoing promotions or discounts that make the service more affordable.
  • Cost Efficiency : Emphasize the low-cost operations and competitive rates compared to other brands.
  • Long-Term Savings : Explain how the quality of service and reliability can save money in the long run by avoiding hidden fees or poor service from competitors.

2. Lack of Trust in the Brand

  • Global Presence : Highlight the brand’s extensive network across multiple countries and its growing reputation.
  • Customer Testimonials : Share positive reviews or case studies from satisfied customers.
  • Professional Support : Emphasize the 24/7 customer service, fully trained team, and IT infrastructure that ensure reliability and professionalism.
  • Transparency : Point to the clear terms and conditions, privacy policy, and cookie policy as evidence of the brand’s commitment to transparency and customer trust.
  • Awards and Recognition : Mention any awards or recognitions the brand has received to build credibility.

If the context does not provide enough information, these arguments can still be used effectively to address the objections.
