Qualification Questions +

Based on the provided context, here are the 5 most relevant and important questions the seller (Surprice Car Rentals) should ask the prospect (Vantage Motor Group) to determine if they are a good fit for the B2B offering of franchise opportunities and partnerships:

  1. What is your current business model, and how do you see a car rental franchise or partnership integrating with your existing operations?
    - This helps assess alignment between Vantage Motor Group's automotive retail business and Surprice's franchise model.
  2. Do you have experience in managing or expanding franchise operations, and what resources (e.g., staff, infrastructure) do you currently have in place?
    - This evaluates the prospect's readiness and capability to take on a franchise.
  3. What are your goals for growth in the next 3-5 years, and how do you envision a partnership with Surprice Car Rentals contributing to those goals?
    - This identifies whether the prospect's growth objectives align with Surprice's offerings.
  4. Are you familiar with the car rental market in your region, and what challenges or opportunities do you foresee in this sector?
    - This gauges the prospect's market knowledge and potential for success in the car rental industry.
  5. What level of support and training would you require from Surprice to ensure the successful launch and operation of a franchise?
    - This helps determine the prospect's expectations and whether Surprice can meet their needs.

These questions are designed to assess the prospect's fit with Surprice's franchise model, their readiness for partnership, and their alignment with Surprice's goals and support structure.

Objection Handling +

1. Cost Concerns

  • Value for Money : Emphasize the competitive pricing and the value provided, such as 24/7 service, multiple insurance options, and a wide range of vehicles.
  • Special Offers : Highlight ongoing promotions or discounts that make the service more affordable.
  • Long-Term Savings : Explain how the quality of service and reliability can save money in the long run by avoiding hidden costs or issues with cheaper alternatives.

2. Lack of Trust in the Brand

  • Global Presence : Mention the extensive network of franchises and operations in multiple countries, showcasing the brand’s reliability and reach.
  • Customer Testimonials : Share positive reviews or case studies from satisfied customers to build credibility.
  • Transparency : Highlight the clear terms and conditions, privacy policy, and customer support available 24/7 to address any concerns.
  • Industry Recognition : Reference any awards or recognitions the brand has received to establish trust.

If the context doesn’t provide enough information, these arguments are still effective for addressing the objections.
