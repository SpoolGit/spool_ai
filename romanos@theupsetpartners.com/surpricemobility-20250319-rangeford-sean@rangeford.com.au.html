Qualification Questions +

Based on the provided context and the seller's business offering (Surprice Car Rentals' B2B franchise opportunities and partnerships), here are the 5 most relevant and important questions to ask the prospect:

  1. What is your current business model, and how do you see a partnership with Surprice Car Rentals enhancing your operations?
    - This helps understand the prospect's business and how they align with Surprice's offerings.
  2. Do you have experience in the car rental or automotive industry, and if so, what has been your focus?
    - This assesses their industry expertise and whether they are a good fit for a franchise or partnership.
  3. What are your goals for expanding or improving your car rental services, and how do you envision Surprice supporting these goals?
    - This identifies their objectives and how Surprice's global network, training, and IT infrastructure can help.
  4. What is your current customer base, and how do you plan to attract new customers under the Surprice brand?
    - This evaluates their market reach and their ability to leverage Surprice's brand and sales network.
  5. What level of investment are you prepared to make, and what support or resources do you expect from Surprice to ensure success?
    - This clarifies their financial commitment and expectations, ensuring alignment with Surprice's franchise model.

These questions are designed to qualify the prospect and determine if they are a good fit for Surprice Car Rentals' B2B offerings.

Objection Handling +

Here are the most relevant arguments the seller can use to overcome the objections:

1. Cost Concerns

  • Value for Money : Emphasize the competitive pricing and value offered, such as 24/7 service, quality vehicles, and optional extras included in the rental package.
  • Special Offers : Highlight any ongoing promotions, discounts, or long-term rental benefits that reduce overall costs.
  • Cost Efficiency : Explain how the service saves money compared to alternatives, such as public transport or other rental companies, by offering transparent pricing and no hidden fees.

2. Lack of Trust in the Brand

  • Global Presence : Mention the extensive network of franchises and operations in multiple countries, showcasing reliability and experience.
  • Customer Support : Highlight the 24/7 customer service and help center, ensuring assistance is always available.
  • Positive Reputation : Reference the brand’s commitment to quality, credibility, and customer satisfaction, supported by testimonials or reviews.
  • Transparency : Point out the clear terms and conditions, privacy policy, and secure payment processes to build trust.

If the context doesn’t provide enough detail, these arguments are general but effective for addressing the objections.
