Objection Handling +

Here are the most relevant arguments the seller can use to overcome the objections of "Cost concerns" and "Lack of trust in the brand" :

1. Cost Concerns

  • Value for Money : Emphasize the competitive pricing and the value-added services included (e.g., 24/7 support, multiple insurance options, and global reach).
  • Special Offers : Highlight ongoing promotions or discounts that make the service more affordable.
  • Long-Term Savings : Explain how the quality of service and reliability can save costs in the long run by avoiding hidden fees or poor service from competitors.

2. Lack of Trust in the Brand

  • Global Presence : Mention the extensive network of franchises and operations in multiple countries, showcasing the brand’s reliability and reach.
  • Customer Support : Highlight the 24/7 customer service and help center, ensuring customers feel supported at all times.
  • Social Proof : Point to active social media presence (Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube) and positive customer reviews to build credibility.
  • Transparency : Reassure customers with clear terms and conditions, privacy policies, and a commitment to quality service.

If the context doesn’t directly address these objections, these arguments are still effective for handling such concerns.
