Qualification Questions +

Based on the provided context, here are the 5 most relevant and important questions the seller (Surprice Car Rentals) should ask the prospect (a motor vehicle manufacturing company with dealerships in the UK) to determine if they are a good fit for franchise opportunities or partnerships:

  1. What is your current business model, and how do you see a partnership or franchise opportunity with Surprice Car Rentals fitting into your operations?
    (This helps assess alignment with the prospect’s business goals and their understanding of the franchise model.)
  2. Do you have experience in the car rental or mobility services industry, and if so, what has been your focus?
    (This evaluates their expertise and whether they can leverage Surprice’s training and support effectively.)
  3. What are your expansion goals, and how do you envision leveraging Surprice’s global sales network and IT infrastructure to achieve them?
    (This identifies their growth ambitions and how Surprice’s resources can support them.)
  4. Are you looking to enhance your existing car rental operations or start a new venture under the Surprice brand?
    (This clarifies whether they are seeking to expand their current business or enter a new market.)
  5. What are your expectations regarding training, support, and operational costs, and how do you plan to manage pricing and profitability in your region?
    (This ensures they understand Surprice’s franchise advantages and are prepared for the partnership’s financial and operational aspects.)

These questions are designed to assess the prospect’s fit with Surprice’s franchise and partnership offerings, their readiness to adopt the brand, and their ability to benefit from Surprice’s global network and support systems.

Objection Handling +

Here are the most relevant arguments the seller can use to overcome the objections of "Cost concerns" and "Lack of trust in the brand" :

1. Cost Concerns

  • Value for Money : Highlight the competitive pricing and the quality of service provided, ensuring customers get the best value for their money.
  • Special Offers : Mention ongoing promotions, discounts, or flexible pricing options to make the service more affordable.
  • Long-Term Savings : Emphasize how the service can save costs in the long run, such as through efficient operations or bundled services.

2. Lack of Trust in the Brand

  • Global Presence : Showcase the brand’s extensive network and presence in multiple countries, demonstrating reliability and experience.
  • Customer Testimonials : Share positive reviews or case studies from satisfied customers to build credibility.
  • 24/7 Support : Highlight the availability of round-the-clock customer service, ensuring customers feel supported at all times.
  • Transparency : Point to clear terms and conditions, privacy policies, and customer care options to build trust.

If the context doesn’t provide enough information, these general strategies can effectively address the objections.
