Qualification Questions +

Based on the provided context and the seller's business offering (franchise opportunities and partnerships with existing car rental companies), here are the 5 most relevant and important questions to ask the prospect:

  1. What is your current business model, and are you interested in expanding or enhancing your car rental operations?
    - This helps determine if the prospect is open to growth or partnership opportunities.
  2. Do you have experience in the car rental industry, and what challenges are you currently facing?
    - This identifies their expertise and pain points, which can be addressed through Surprice's offerings.
  3. Are you looking to leverage a global sales network and established brand to increase your market presence?
    - This assesses their interest in Surprice's global network and brand recognition.
  4. What is your budget and investment capacity for franchise opportunities or partnerships?
    - This helps gauge their financial readiness for collaboration.
  5. How important is IT infrastructure, training, and ongoing support for your business operations?
    - This evaluates their need for Surprice's comprehensive support system, including IT and training.

These questions are designed to qualify the prospect's fit for Surprice Car Rentals' B2B offerings and identify opportunities for collaboration.

Objection Handling +

Here are the most relevant arguments the seller can use to overcome the objections:

  1. Cost Concerns:

    • Highlight the value for money by emphasizing the quality of service, optimal pricing, and the inclusion of multiple insurance schemes.
    • Mention special offers and discounts that make the service more affordable.
    • Stress the 24/7 service and global reach, which ensures convenience and reliability, justifying the cost.

  2. Lack of Trust in the Brand:

    • Showcase the global presence and widespread network in multiple countries, demonstrating reliability and experience.
    • Emphasize the quality of service and the expertise of the Surprice team, which is focused on customer satisfaction.
    • Provide social proof by directing the customer to their active social media presence (Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube) and positive reviews or testimonials.
    • Mention the transparency in terms of use, privacy policy, and cookie policy, which builds credibility.

These arguments address the objections directly by focusing on value, trust, and reliability.
